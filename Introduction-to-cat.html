Introduction to Computer Applications Technology (CAT)

Computer Applications Technology (CAT) is a subject that equips students with the necessary skills to effectively use technology in their everyday lives. This subject focuses on the practical application of computer hardware and software to solve problems, communicate, and manage information.

CAPS Curriculum Alignment

The CAPS (Curriculum and Assessment Policy Statement) curriculum for CAT covers the following key areas:

  • Introduction to Computers
  • Hardware and Software
  • Networks and Internet
  • Word Processing
  • Spreadsheets
  • Databases
  • Presentations
  • Information Management
  • Social Implications of ICT

IEB Curriculum Alignment

The IEB (Independent Examinations Board) curriculum for CAT includes the following focus areas:

  • Computer Hardware and System Software
  • Networks and Communication Technologies
  • Information Systems
  • Advanced Word Processing
  • Data Manipulation and Analysis using Spreadsheets
  • Database Management
  • Multimedia and Web Development
  • Programming Basics
  • Impact of ICT on Society
