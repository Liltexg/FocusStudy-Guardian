Internet and IoT - Grade 12 CAT

1. Internet Communication

The Internet is a global network that allows for communication and data exchange. Key aspects include:

  • Protocols: Rules for data transmission, including HTTP, HTTPS, FTP, and TCP/IP.
  • Web Services: Services provided over the web, such as online banking and email.
  • Network Infrastructure: The hardware and software needed to maintain the internet, including servers, routers, and cables.
  • Data Transmission: How data is sent and received over the internet, including packet switching and bandwidth considerations.

2. Online Transactions

Online transactions involve the exchange of goods and services over the internet. Important concepts include:

  • E-commerce: Buying and selling products or services online through platforms like Amazon and eBay.
  • E-banking: Managing bank accounts and conducting financial transactions via online banking systems.
  • Secure Transactions: Methods for ensuring the security of online transactions, such as encryption and secure payment gateways.

3. The Internet of Things (IoT)

The Internet of Things (IoT) refers to the network of connected devices that can communicate and share data. Key points include:

  • Definition: Devices that are connected to the internet and can interact with other devices or systems.
  • Examples: Smart home devices like thermostats, lights, and security cameras.
  • Applications: Uses of IoT in various fields such as healthcare, agriculture, and smart cities.
  • Challenges: Issues related to security, privacy, and interoperability in IoT systems.

4. The Fourth Industrial Revolution (4IR)

The Fourth Industrial Revolution involves the integration of advanced technologies into various aspects of life and industry:

  • Technologies: Includes AI, robotics, and IoT.
  • Impact: How these technologies are transforming industries and economies.
  • Opportunities: Benefits of adopting these technologies, including increased efficiency and new business models.
  • Challenges: Potential issues such as job displacement and ethical concerns.

5. Social Media

Social media platforms enable users to create, share, and interact with content. Important aspects include:

  • Platforms: Examples include Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, and LinkedIn.
  • Usage: How individuals and businesses use social media for communication, marketing, and networking.
  • Impact: Effects of social media on society, including both positive and negative aspects.

6. Internet Access

Access to the internet is crucial for digital connectivity. Key factors include:

  • Types of Access: Includes broadband, fiber-optic, and mobile data.
  • Coverage: Availability of internet services in different geographic regions.
  • Speeds: Differences in internet speed and performance depending on the type of connection.

7. Browser and Email Software

Browsers and email software are essential tools for navigating the internet and communication:

  • Browsers: Software for accessing websites, such as Chrome, Firefox, and Safari.
  • Email Software: Applications for managing email, including Gmail, Outlook, and Yahoo Mail.
  • Features: Key functionalities of browsers and email software, including security features and customization options.

8. Usability of Web Pages and Websites

Ensuring that web pages and websites are user-friendly and accessible:

  • Design Principles: Principles such as simplicity, consistency, and clarity.
  • Accessibility: Ensuring web content is accessible to users with disabilities.
  • Performance: Factors affecting website speed and responsiveness.
