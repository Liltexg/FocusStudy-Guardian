Online Security - Grade 12 CAT

1. Social-Engineering Tricks

Social-engineering involves manipulating individuals into divulging confidential information. Common tricks include:

  • Phishing: Fake emails or messages that appear to be from legitimate sources, asking for personal information.
  • Pretexting: Creating a fabricated scenario to obtain information from individuals.
  • Baiting: Offering something enticing to lure victims into providing sensitive information or downloading malicious software.
  • Impersonation: Pretending to be someone the target knows or trusts to gain access to information.

2. Data Protection

Protecting data involves safeguarding both digital and physical information. Key practices include:

  • Encryption: Converting data into a secure format that can only be read by authorized individuals.
  • Secure Passwords: Using strong, unique passwords and changing them regularly.
  • Regular Backups: Creating backups of important data to recover from data loss or breaches.
  • Access Controls: Limiting access to sensitive information to authorized users only.

3. Protecting Yourself Online

To stay safe online, consider these protective measures:

  • Verify Sources: Confirm the legitimacy of websites and communications before sharing personal information.
  • Use Two-Factor Authentication: Add an extra layer of security by requiring two forms of verification to access accounts.
  • Update Software: Keep operating systems and applications up-to-date to protect against known vulnerabilities.
  • Be Cautious with Personal Information: Avoid sharing excessive personal information on social media and other platforms.

4. E-commerce and E-banking

When engaging in online transactions, ensure that:

  • Secure Connections: Use secure websites (HTTPS) for online purchases and banking.
  • Monitor Accounts: Regularly check bank and credit card statements for unauthorized transactions.
  • Use Trusted Payment Gateways: Opt for well-known and secure payment processors.
  • Beware of Scams: Watch out for fraudulent schemes and phishing attempts related to online transactions.

5. How Antivirus Programs Work

Antivirus programs help protect computers from malware and other threats by:

  • Scanning Files: Checking files and programs for known viruses and malware signatures.
  • Real-Time Protection: Monitoring system activities for suspicious behavior and preventing infections.
  • Updating Definitions: Regularly updating virus definitions to recognize new threats.
  • Removing Threats: Quarantining or removing malicious files and software from the system.

6. Social Media Security

To maintain security on social media platforms:

  • Adjust Privacy Settings: Configure account settings to limit visibility to your posts and personal information.
  • Be Wary of Links: Avoid clicking on suspicious links or downloading attachments from unknown sources.
  • Report Suspicious Activity: Notify the platform of any suspicious or inappropriate behavior.
